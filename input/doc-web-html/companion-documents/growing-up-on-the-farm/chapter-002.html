She taught us all our nursery rhymes
Made sure that we could spell.
Was interested in what we did
Made sure we did it well.

Beneath the coal oil lamp at night,
She'd guide us in assignments.
She'd quizz us for the test next day
Made sure we knew the refinements.

When public speaking came around,
She helped me to compose.
Without her help I never won
AT Zenon Park it really showed.

With Dad another story came;
He loved to play with numbers.
How much is 9 times 6 plus 8
Less than that you didn't slumber.

Because of all his drills at home
For accuracy and speed;
I won a bag of peanuts
The day I took the lead.

SUMMER DAYS

The summer had its ups and downs;
We grumbled lots I know.
It didn't have the magic
That winter had with snow.

The morning saw us picking stones
From 8 till 9 or so
Then we'd make hay or stook the fields
Till supper, then we'd slow.

The next day was the same as that
At least that's how it seemed.
While all our friends had loads of fun
Swimming in OUR creek.

Saturdays were different
Throughout the summertime.
We'd always quit work early,
Toss in the bindertwine.

The house was cleaned from end to end.
The floors shone with a glitter.
We all had baths and cleaned our shoes
We looked like a clean litter.
